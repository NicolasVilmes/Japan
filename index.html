JAPAN

Japan is an island country in the Pacific Ocean with a population of over 126 million people, making it the world’s tenth most populous country. The country is divided into 47 prefectures in eight regions, with Hokkaido being the northernmost prefecture and Okinawa being the southernmost. The capital of Japan is Tokyo, which is also the country’s largest city. The Greater Tokyo Area is the most populous metropolitan area in the world.

POLITIC & ECONOMY

POLITIC

The political system of Japan is a constitutional monarchy in which the power of the Emperor is limited and is relegated primarily to ceremonial duties. As a ceremonial figurehead, he is defined by the Constitution to be the symbol of the State and of the unity of the people. Power is held chiefly by the Prime Minister of Japan and other elected members of the National Diet, which is the country's legislature. The judiciary is independent of the executive and the legislature.

ECONOMY

ECONOMIC ACTIVITIES

Japan is a great reference in the export and trade of products, being a great pillar of the economy. Among its activities, the import of agricultural products stands out: rice, vegetables, fruits, sugar beet, fish and eggs. And also industrialized products: automobiles, machines, computers, electronic equipment, ships, chemical products, textiles and processed foods.
The currency in circulation in the country is the Yen (¥ / 円) (JPY). Also, its HDI is a total of 0.884, with 1,36 births per woman of birth rate and 11,10% of mortality rate.
